About Me

I am a PhD student specializing in condensed matter physics, with a deep fascination for the fundamental properties of quantum materials and their emergent phenomena. My research focuses on understanding the electronic, magnetic, and optical properties of novel materials at low temperatures and under extreme conditions.

Beyond experimental physics, I have developed a strong interest in algorithmic tools and computational methods that can enhance our understanding of complex physical systems. I enjoy exploring the intersection between theoretical physics and advanced computational techniques, particularly in areas such as machine learning for materials discovery, quantum algorithms, and numerical simulations.

This combination of experimental expertise and computational skills allows me to approach scientific problems from multiple angles and develop innovative solutions for complex research challenges.

Research Interests

  • Quantum Materials: Investigating electronic properties of 2D materials, topological insulators, and superconductors
  • Low-Temperature Physics: Exploring quantum phase transitions and many-body phenomena at millikelvin temperatures
  • Magnetism & Spintronics: Studying magnetic ordering and spin-dependent transport in quantum materials
  • Experimental Techniques: Advanced measurements using SQUID magnetometry, transport measurements, and optical spectroscopy
